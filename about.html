Why Mediation?

Mediation is a voluntary, confidential process in which a neutral third party helps the parties resolve their disputes. As a mediator, Sarah has training and experience in techniques that facilitate the parties evaluation of their issues and options in order to achieve a settlement.

As part of the mediation process, each party can provide the mediator with mediation statements and documents that present the partys positions and interests. The mediator may communicate with the parties prior to the mediation session to obtain additional information or discuss the details of the mediation session. During the mediation session, the mediator may meet separately with each party, as well as bringing the parties together to engage in settlement negotiations. If a settlement is reached, the parties will put the terms of the settlement in a written binding agreement.

As an attorney, Sarah values the mediation process. As a mediator, she enjoys the opportunity to assist the parties in arriving at voluntary and mutually agreeable resolutions. The mediation process may avoid the need for costly and time-consuming litigation in which a judge decides the outcome.
